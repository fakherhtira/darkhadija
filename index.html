About the museum

At Dar Khadija Museum, we celebrate creativity, openness, tolerance,and generosity. We aim to be inclusive places—both onsite and online where diverse cultural, artistic, social, and political positions are welcome. We’re committed to sharing the culture and traditions of Cap-Bon, and hope you will join us in exploring the art, ideas, and issues of our time.

The museum is located in a traditional house in the medina of Hammamet. It was inaugurated in 2008.

The DAR KHADIJA MUSEUM has opened its doors to the public to exhibit stagings that illustrate and highlight Tunisian Heritage (everyday scenes of weaving, embroidery, distillation of orange blossoms, wedding ceremonies, fishing,. ..) In short, The history of traditional Cap_Bon inheritances in the heart of the old town of Hammamet between the Phoenicians, the Romans, the Knights of Malta, the French protectorate

The fort of hammamet

This fort is one of the many military fortifications scattered along the Tunisian coast to defend the coastline. The borj – bastion – was built in 893. In around 1463 it was considerably extended to serve as the residence of the city governor as well. At the end of the XVIth century, it was substantially modified and adapted to accommodate firearms. The monument was turned into barracks after the establishment of the French protectorate over Tunisian in 1881.

History of the museum

View our history

Originally DAR KHADIJA is a Traditional House acquired by a son to give it to his mother Khadija. The Museum offers and stages the history of the city of Hammamet and the Cap-Bon region through centuries and civilizations.

2008

Moving in

The opening

After the death of his mother, the son transformed The Three-story House into a Museum in memory of his mother. And it was on March 18, 2008, that the DAR KHADIJA MUSEUM opened its doors to the public to exhibit stagings that illustrate and highlight Tunisian Heritage.
